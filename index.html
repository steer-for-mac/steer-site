What it does

One menu-bar app, every controller surface.

Every button, stick, touchpad and gyro the hardware exposes — wired up to anything macOS understands.

Radial app launcher

L3 opens an 8-app ring. Aim with the left stick, click a face button to launch.

On-screen keyboard

Daisy-wheel layout reachable from any controller. Type without a keyboard nearby.

Menu-bar resident

No dock icon, no main window. Battery, profile and setup wizard one click away.

Cursor & scroll

Left stick aims; right stick scrolls. Curve, dead zone and acceleration are user-shaped.

Keyboard & shortcuts

Any controller button to any keystroke, including ⌘⇧⌥⌃ chords. Six modifier layers per profile.

Adaptive triggers

Five DualSense presets — gun click, bow tension, brake — or hand-tune the 0–255 sliders.

Gyro mouse

Tilt the controller to aim. Hold R3 to engage; release to suspend bias drift.

Per-app profiles

Steer loads the right binding set when you switch apps. Safari to Xcode is one ⌘-tab away.

Haptics & LED

Custom feedback for scroll ticks, layer toggles, volume boundaries. Per-layer LED colors.

Trust

Nothing happens without you seeing it first.

Steer shows you exactly what a community preset does before you accept it, and keeps a full restore history so every change is reversible. Imported macros and presets land in a review popover, not directly in your bindings.

FAQ

Boring details, answered.

Why does it need Accessibility?

To post mouse and keyboard events to other apps. macOS gates synthetic input behind Accessibility for everything from TextExpander to BetterTouchTool. Steer asks once; nothing leaves your machine.

Will it break on the next macOS?

I run Steer on the developer betas every cycle. Updates ship on the same day macOS does — both via Sparkle and the App Store. If something does break, email me; turnaround is hours, not weeks.

What controllers are supported?

DualSense, DualSense Edge, DualShock 4, Xbox Series, Xbox One, Xbox Elite, Switch Pro, and any Made-for-iPhone controller. Wired or Bluetooth, both work.

Input Monitoring — do I need it?

Only if you have a DualSense Edge and want to bind its two rear paddles. Every other controller works with Accessibility alone.

App Store or direct — which?

Same binary, same updates. MAS is convenient and handles family sharing; direct gets you the trial, ships an hour sooner on update day, and supports the project more directly.

What does it not do?

Mac-to-Mac relay, Xbox Elite firmware paddle modes, controller-as-MIDI. If those are deal-breakers, ControllerKeys is the right pick.
